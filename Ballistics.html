Ballistic Plate Protection Levels

Protection Level Description Caliber Protection Capability
Level I Protects against small-caliber handguns (.22LR) .22LR, .380 ACP Low-level handgun rounds
Level IIA Protects against 9mm FMJ and .40 S&W FMJ 9mm, .40 S&W, .45 ACP Standard handgun rounds
Level II Protects against 9mm FMJ and .357 Magnum JSP 9mm, .357 Magnum Standard handgun rounds
Level IIIA Protects against .44 Magnum SJHP and .357 SIG FMJ .44 Magnum, .357 SIG High-velocity handgun rounds
Level III Protects against 7.62x51mm NATO (.308 Winchester) 7.62x51mm NATO Rifle rounds
Level IV Protects against armor-piercing rifle rounds .30-06 AP, 7.62x51mm NATO AP High-powered rifle rounds

Notes:

  • Protection levels are based on the National Institute of Justice (NIJ) standard 0101.06 for ballistic resistance of body armor.
  • Each protection level is designed to withstand specific ballistic threats, with higher levels offering greater protection against more powerful ammunition.
  • Rifle rounds refer to high-velocity ammunition typically fired from rifles, such as the 7.62x51mm NATO (.308 Winchester) and .30-06 armor-piercing (AP) rounds.
